FAQ

Broadcast Frequency: Echoes of the Fall

Q: What is Broadcast Frequency: Echoes of the Fall?
It’s a post-apocalyptic survival simulation that runs in your Twitch chat. Viewers join as survivors and the AI runs all the action—events, loot, injuries, death, extraction, and more. It’s hands-off for the streamer, and mostly automated for the chat.
Q: How do viewers play?
Viewers type !transmit in chat to join. Once inside the Zone, the AI handles survival, danger, and discovery. Viewers get updates about their fate as the simulation ticks along.
Q: What commands can viewers use?
Basic survival is automated, but viewers can use a few commands:
  • !transmit — join the simulation
  • !extract — attempt to escape
  • !vault — bank items
  • !status — check on their survivor
More commands may be added, but overall viewer interaction is limited—this is more of a simulation to watch unfold than a game to control.
Q: Does the streamer have to do anything?
No manual work required. Start the bot, let the game run, and enjoy the chaos in chat as your viewers struggle to survive.
Q: How does this help streamers?
It keeps your chat active and your content fresh with unique, automated stories—no micromanagement needed. Viewers stay engaged watching their fate unfold, and future features will reward subs/bits and drive new viewers through cross-stream events.
Q: Will this spam my chat?
The sim filters out most noise and only posts important events to chat. With many players, it can get busy—but new tools are being added, including per-player DMs and streamer controls, to keep your chat clean.
Q: Is there PvP?
Not yet—but player-vs-player features are coming in future updates. For now, all threats are from the environment.
Q: How do I get access?
Closed Alpha only. DM on Twitter or head to broadcastfrequency.com to sign up for early access.
Q: Is this free?
The Alpha is free for selected testers and streamers. The final version will always have a free way to play, with optional premium features down the road.
Q: What’s planned for the future?
More archetypes, new event types, Twitch Bits/sub integration, PvP, cross-channel events, more ways for viewers and streamers to influence the simulation, and chat DM options to reduce clutter.
